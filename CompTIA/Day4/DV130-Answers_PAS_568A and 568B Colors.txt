25_SAS_568A and 568B Colors
Self-Assessment Sheet

Q1. What are the international standards for cabling? 
Ans: ISO/IEC 11801


Q2. The TIA standard for cabling is the _________standard, which is the ___________________Standard.
Ans. TIA 568 Standard, Commercial Building Telecommunications Cabling

Q3. What does TIA stand for?
Ans. Telecommunications Industry Association

Q4. What does the TIA 568 standard cover? 
Ans: TIA 568 Standard covers what color wires you use on what pins of an ethernet connection. This is referred to as the pin and pair assignments of eight-conductor 100-ohm balanced twisted pair cabling. And as an abbreviation, we often just refer to this as T568A and T568B.


Q5. What are the two standards for 8P8C connectors? 
Ans: T568A & T568B

Q6. What are 8P8C connectors?
Ans. These are 8 position 8 conductor connectors.


Q7. What is the 568A coloring scheme associated with? 
Ans: 568A coloring scheme is associated with Horizontal cabling.

Q8. What is the difference between the 568A and the 568B color standards? 
Ans: White and Green and Green versus white and Orange and Orange.

Q9. : What color scheme do ethernet crossover cables use? 
Ans: 568A on one end and 568B on another end.

Q10. What do the 568A and 568B standards specify? 
Ans. These two standards provide us with the colors that we’ll use for our 8P8C connectors. These are 8 position 8 conductor connectors.


Q11. What is the difference between T568A and T568B? 
Ans: The only difference between T568A and T568B is that the orange and green pairs are interchanged.

Q12. How are pins 1 and 2 different between the 568A and 568B standards? 
Ans: 568A - Pins 1 and 2 are White Green and Green ones.
         568B - Pins 1 and 2 are White Orange and Orange ones.

Q13. What is the best way to tell what standard is in use for an ethernet cable? 
Ans: By looking at the back of an Ethernet cable and see exactly what colors might be on this particular pin out.

Q14. What are the colors in the A and B standard? 
Ans: White and Green, Green, White and Orange, Orange, White and Blue, Blue & White and Brown and Brown.

Q15. Write an example of a block that has a  different color assigned to A & B standard.
Ans. Punch Down Block.
